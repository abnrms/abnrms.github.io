My Website

Hi! My name is Hamlet, and I just recently graduated from the University of Arizona with a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science and a minor in Statistics and Data Science. I graduated in December 2025 with summa cum laude distinction, along with some teaching and research assistant experience at my university.

My professional experience lies mainly in web design, as I worked in Cloud Microphones' marketing team to help revamp their website with an e-commerce mindset. During my time at Cloud, I also experimented with graphics-based C programming, creating a well-designed standalone game to be displayed in a trade show environment.

I've served as a teaching assistant for three computer science courses at UA: Intro to Discrete Mathematics, Analysis of Discrete Structures, and Systems Programming & Unix. My work included holding regularly scheduled office hours, grading and reviewing assignments and exams, holding supplemental instruction sessions, and handling student questions both in class and during comprehensive review sessions before exams.

My undergraduate research at UA—with Dr. Reyan Ahmed, Dr. Stephen Kobourov, Dr. Cesim Erten, Jacob Miller, and Jonah Lotz— centered a graph-theoretical critique of the scale-(in)variance of different stress metrics used in graph layouts. In this project, I conducted empirical analysis over a suite of 2000+ graphs, generating several cohesive tables and scatterplots of experimental results and runtime analysis.

This website will serve as a hub for my professional information, personal projects, and anything else I decide to add to it!
